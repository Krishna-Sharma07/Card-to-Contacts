# Privacy Policy for Card to Contacts

_Last updated: 2026-07-14_

Card to Contacts is designed to work entirely on your device. This policy
describes what the app does and does not do with your data.

## What the app does

- **Camera / photo library access** is used only to let you capture or pick
  a photo of a business card. Photos never leave your device.
- **On-device text recognition** (Google ML Kit) reads the text on that
  photo directly on your device. No image or recognized text is uploaded
  anywhere -- the recognition model ships inside the app itself.
- **Contacts access** is used only when you tap "Save to Contacts," to
  create or update an entry in your device's own contacts, and to check
  whether a contact with the same phone number already exists so you don't
  end up with duplicates.
- **Scan history** (name, company, and the other fields you saved) is
  stored locally on your device using standard app storage, so you can
  revisit or delete past scans. It is never transmitted anywhere.

## What the app does not do

- It does not use the internet. The release build requests no network
  permission at all.
- It does not use analytics, crash reporting, ads, or any third-party SDKs
  that transmit data off your device.
- It does not create an account, and there are no servers involved -- there
  is nothing for us to collect, store remotely, or lose in a breach.

## Deleting your data

You can remove individual scan history entries or clear all of it from the
History screen at any time. Uninstalling the app removes everything the app
itself stored. Contacts you saved to your device's address book are
unaffected by uninstalling the app, since they belong to your device's
Contacts app, not to Card to Contacts.
